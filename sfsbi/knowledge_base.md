## Medical Weight Loss
Medical weight loss is a doctor-supervised approach to losing weight safely and effectively using science-backed treatments and expert nutritional guidance.

GLP-1 medications are one of our primary medical weight loss treatments. They help control hunger and regulate blood sugar by slowing digestion, reducing appetite, and improving how the body processes food. We offer both oral and injectable GLP-1 medications as well as specially formulated compounded GLP-1s made in high-quality compounding pharmacies for customized dosing. Important note — we refer to these medications as GLP-1 medications or weight loss medications in general terms. We do not advertise specific brand-name medications.

We also prescribe phentermine and phendimetrazine, which are FDA-approved appetite suppressants that reduce cravings and make it easier to stick to a healthy eating plan. Typical monthly costs for medical weight loss programs range from around two hundred to five hundred dollars per month depending on the medication and plan.

Additionally, we offer intramuscular vitamin B12 injections to boost energy and metabolism, and MIC fat burner injections containing methionine, inositol, and choline to help break down fat and support liver function.

## Bariatric Surgery Options
We offer several surgical weight loss procedures. All surgical packages are all-inclusive, meaning the price covers the surgeon, facility, anesthesia, pathology, and follow-up care for six to twelve months on average. Surgical packages typically range from around two thousand to eight thousand dollars per patient depending on the procedure.

The Orbera Intragastric Balloon System is a temporary, nonsurgical weight loss tool placed through an endoscopic procedure with no incisions. The balloon sits in the stomach for six months helping patients feel fuller faster, then it is removed endoscopically.

The Allurion Gastric Balloon is our newest offering — a swallowable gastric balloon that requires no surgery, no anesthesia, and no endoscopy for placement or removal. The patient simply swallows a capsule in the office, the balloon is filled with liquid, and it stays in the stomach for approximately sixteen weeks. After about four months it naturally deflates and passes through the digestive system on its own. The program includes personalized coaching and medical oversight.

The Lap-Band System is an adjustable gastric band placed around the upper stomach that creates a smaller pouch. The band can be tightened, loosened, or removed as needed.

Apollo Overstitch ESG or endoscopic sleeve gastroplasty is a nonsurgical procedure that reduces stomach size using an endoscopic suturing device instead of removing part of the stomach.

Gastric sleeve surgery, also called sleeve gastrectomy, permanently removes about eighty percent of the stomach and reduces hunger hormone production. This is for patients who qualify based on BMI.

Gastric bypass or Roux-en-Y creates a small stomach pouch connected directly to the small intestine, limiting food intake and nutrient absorption. This is typically for patients with a BMI of forty or higher, or thirty-five or higher with obesity-related health conditions.

Transoral outlet reduction or TORe is a minimally invasive procedure for patients who have regained weight after gastric bypass. It uses sutures placed through the mouth to tighten the enlarged outlet between the stomach pouch and small intestine.

## BMI Qualification Guidelines
For bariatric surgery, general BMI guidelines are as follows. A BMI of thirty or higher may qualify for nonsurgical options like the Orbera balloon, Allurion balloon, or Apollo ESG. A BMI of thirty-five or higher with obesity-related health conditions like diabetes, high blood pressure, or sleep apnea may qualify for gastric sleeve or gastric bypass. A BMI of forty or higher generally qualifies for any bariatric surgery option. We offer a free consultation to help patients understand which procedure is right for them based on their specific health situation. Patients can calculate their BMI at the American Society for Metabolic and Bariatric Surgery calculator at asmbs.org.

## CoolSculpting
CoolSculpting is a nonsurgical fat loss treatment that uses cryolipolysis technology to freeze and permanently destroy fat cells without surgery or downtime. It can eliminate up to twenty-five percent of fat cells in the treatment area.

We treat multiple areas including double chin, bra fat, upper arms or bat wings, upper and lower back, abdomen, love handles, thighs, banana roll under the buttocks, and above the knees.

We also offer dual sculpting, which uses two CoolSculpting devices at the same time to treat two areas simultaneously, cutting treatment time in half. Each treatment session takes about an hour. Results begin to show within one to three months with final results at about six months.

CoolSculpting is not a weight loss procedure. It is best for patients at or near their goal weight who want to target stubborn fat pockets. We offer a free initial CoolSculpting consultation.
